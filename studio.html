Inside Eclipse

Our Studio

How we work, what we use, and why we do it the way we do.

Philosophy
"We don't make games to sell units. We make games because somewhere out there, the player who needs that exact world exists — and it's our job to build it for them."
— Soren Keller, Creative Director
Process

How We Build

01

Dream

Every project starts with an emotion we want the player to feel. Not a mechanic, not a monetisation model — a feeling. We prototype rapidly, throw most of it away, and keep what feels true.

02

Build

Iteration over perfection. We ship vertical slices to our community early and often, integrate feedback ruthlessly, and keep a flat hierarchy so good ideas come from anywhere.

03

Polish

The last 20% is 80% of what players remember. We dedicate the final phase entirely to feel — animations, audio, transitions, feedback loops. This is where games go from good to unforgettable.

04

Ship & Support

Shipping is the beginning, not the end. We patch, update, and listen. Our community Discord is where we live after launch.

Workspace

Berlin Studio

Our physical studio is a 200m² converted warehouse in Berlin-Kreuzberg, packed with reference books, concept art pinboards, a dedicated sound booth, and an embarrassing amount of action figures.

But we're also fully remote-capable — half the team works from home, and our async culture means great work happens across all time zones.
